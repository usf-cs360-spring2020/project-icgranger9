Data

My dataset is provided to me by the International Peace Research Institute. As mentioned elsewhere, it contains information on every major international arms deal, going back to 1950. While I cannot easily see the size of the entire dataset, when looking at just arms deals supplied by the United state since 2000, there is around 400kb of data. The dataset itself is available here. The license is as follows:

Fair use of SIPRI content is defined as

  • the excerption of SIPRI copyrighted material for such purposes as criticism, comment, news reporting, teaching, scholarship or research in which the use is for non-commercial purposes
  • the reproduction of less than 10 per cent of a published data set.
NB: Both the above conditions must apply to count as fair use.

In working with this dataset, I believe that there will be a couple of issues that I will have to overcome. Namely, there are two main ways to interact with this data, one of which allows you to get aggregate information on all Suppliers / Recipients. However, to get more detailed information, I believe I will need to use their other tool, which allows me to get details about each individual transfer for a given Supplier / Recipient. To get the more detailed information that I'd like to use in my visualization, I may have look at each relevant transaction record. However, this does add another layer of difficulties to the visualization. This data is given to me in a rtf format, instead of a more traditional csv. So, I will likely need to do a fair about of data cleaning to get all the relevant information.

Wrangling

No wrangling has been preformed on the data yet, however I am expecting to have to preform some sort of data cleaning and formatting to make the data easier to work with. Once I have more details, I will fill this section out further.
